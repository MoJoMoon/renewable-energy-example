üåä How Tidal Forces Work

This simulation shows how the Moon's gravitational pull creates two tidal bulges on Earth - one facing the Moon and one on the opposite side. As Earth rotates, different locations experience high and low tides.

üéØ Gravitational Pull

The Moon pulls more strongly on the side of Earth facing it, creating a bulge of water toward the Moon.

üîÑ Opposite Bulge

The far side of Earth experiences less gravitational pull, allowing water to bulge outward due to centrifugal force.

üåç Earth's Rotation

As Earth rotates every 24 hours, locations move through both tidal bulges, experiencing two high tides daily.

üïí Tidal Timing

The Moon rises about 50 minutes later each day, so tides also shift by about 50 minutes daily.
